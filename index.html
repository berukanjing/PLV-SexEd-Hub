STI Awareness & Prevention

Sexually transmitted infections are more common than you think — and most are preventable or treatable. Understanding how they spread is the first step.

HIV/AIDSChlamydia GonorrheaHerpes HPVSyphilis

Key Prevention Methods

  • ✅ Use condoms consistently and correctly
  • ✅ Get regularly tested — especially if sexually active
  • ✅ Limit number of sexual partners
  • ✅ Vaccinate against HPV and Hepatitis B
  • ✅ Open communication with your partner(s)

Consent & Healthy Relationships

Consent isn't just a "yes" — it's ongoing, enthusiastic, and can be withdrawn anytime. Healthy relationships are built on mutual respect and clear communication.

Quick Facts

Things Every PLV Student Should Know

Short, sharp, and important. Scroll through these facts.

🧬

STIs Can Be Asymptomatic

Many STIs show no symptoms at all. The only way to know for sure is to get tested — not to assume you're fine because you feel okay.

💊

HIV Is Now Manageable

With proper antiretroviral treatment, people living with HIV can live long, healthy lives and prevent transmission to others.

🚫

"No" Is a Complete Sentence

You don't need to explain, justify, or apologize for saying no. Consent that is given under pressure is not real consent.

🩺

Get Tested Regularly

If you're sexually active, regular STI testing is part of good health. Testing is confidential and available at PLV's clinic.

🤐

Silence Is Not Consent

Not saying "no" doesn't mean "yes." Consent must be clear, enthusiastic, and freely given — every single time.

💬

Talk to Your Partner

Open conversations about boundaries, testing status, and protection are signs of maturity and care — not awkwardness.

Understanding Consent

Consent Is FRIES

A simple framework used worldwide to understand what real consent looks like.

F
Freely Given
Consent is given without pressure, manipulation, or fear. It's a genuine choice made by someone in control.
R
Reversible
Anyone can change their mind at any time — even if they said yes before, even mid-activity. That must always be respected.
I
Informed
Both people must know what they're agreeing to. Lying or withholding information to get a "yes" is not real consent.
E
Enthusiastic
Look for an enthusiastic yes! Consent is about wanting to, not just going along with something reluctantly.
S
Specific
Saying yes to one thing doesn't mean yes to everything. Each new activity needs its own clear consent.
🔍 Myth Busters

Fact-Check Your Beliefs

Misconceptions about sexual health are dangerous. Let's clear them up.

"You can tell if someone has an STI just by looking at them."

❌ MYTH
+
✔ THE TRUTH

Most STIs have no visible symptoms at all. The only reliable way to know is through proper medical testing. Never assume someone's status based on appearance.

"Condoms are 100% effective against all STIs."

❌ MYTH
+
✔ THE TRUTH

Condoms are highly effective but not 100%. They greatly reduce the risk of HIV and most STIs, but some infections like HPV or herpes can spread through skin contact not covered by a condom. Still — always use one!

"If my partner doesn't say 'no,' it means they're okay with it."

❌ MYTH
+
✔ THE TRUTH

Consent must be clearly communicated, not assumed from silence. Some people freeze or feel unable to speak up. Always check in and look for enthusiastic, willing participation.

"STIs only happen to people with many partners."

❌ MYTH
+
✔ THE TRUTH

Anyone who is sexually active can contract an STI — it only takes one unprotected encounter. STIs don't discriminate based on how many partners someone has had.

"If you've already had sex, you can't say no the next time."

❌ MYTH
+
✔ THE TRUTH

Past consent never obligates future consent. Every encounter requires new, enthusiastic consent. Regardless of your history with someone, you always have the right to say no.

"HIV can be transmitted through mosquito bites or casual contact."

❌ MYTH
+
✔ THE TRUTH

HIV is transmitted through specific bodily fluids: blood, semen, vaginal fluids, and breast milk. You cannot get HIV from mosquitoes, hugging, sharing food, or casual everyday contact.

Call the Department of Health at 1555 for health advice, STI information, and referrals to nearby testing centers.

Love Yourself Foundation

A Philippine-based NGO offering free HIV testing, counseling, and support services for young people across Metro Manila.

PLV students can message the PLV Guidance & Counseling Office for private, student-centered support on relationships and sexual health.

📖

HealthDev PH

A Filipino youth health information platform with articles, guides, and Q&As on sexual health written in Filipino and English.
